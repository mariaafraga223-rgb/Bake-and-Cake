Walnut & Chocolate Cookies

Nutty brown-butter flavor, crunchy toasted walnuts, and melty chocolate — rich but perfectly balanced.

Prep 20 min | Cook 12–13 min | Total 1 hr 15 min | Servings 12 cookies

Ingredients


  • 117 g walnuts, roughly chopped
  • 170 g unsalted butter
  • 200 g packed light brown sugar
  • 50 g granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg + 1 large egg yolk (room temperature)
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • 219 g all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 tsp baking soda
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 224 g semisweet chocolate chips (or chopped chocolate)
  • Flaky salt (optional, for topping)

Steps


  1. Toast the walnuts: preheat the oven to 190°C and line a small tray with parchment paper.
  2. Spread walnuts in an even layer and bake for 5–7 minutes, stirring halfway. Cool completely.
  3. Brown the butter: melt butter in a saucepan over medium-low heat, stirring and scraping often, until golden with brown bits (about 5–7 minutes). Let cool a few minutes.
  4. In a bowl, mix brown butter with brown sugar and granulated sugar until combined.
  5. Add egg, egg yolk, and vanilla. Mix well.
  6. In a separate bowl, combine flour, baking soda, and salt.
  7. Add dry ingredients to the wet mixture and fold until no flour streaks remain (don’t overmix).
  8. Fold in chocolate chips and toasted walnuts.
  9. Chill the dough for 30 minutes. Scoop portions, then chill again for at least 30 minutes (or up to 24 hours).
  10. Preheat the oven to 180°C. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
  11. Place dough balls on trays, spaced apart. Bake 12–13 minutes, until edges are golden but centers are still soft.
  12. Sprinkle with flaky salt if using. Cool slightly on the tray before serving.
