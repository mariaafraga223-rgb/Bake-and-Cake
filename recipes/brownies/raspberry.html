Raspberry Brownies

Deep cocoa brownies with pockets of fresh raspberry — rich chocolate, bright fruit, perfect contrast.

Prep 15 min | Cook 25–35 min | Total 50 min | Servings 9 squares

Ingredients


  • 170 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 65 g Dutch-process cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 350 g light brown sugar (packed)
  • 3 large eggs
  • 94 g all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 62 g fresh raspberries (or frozen)
  • 112 g semisweet or bittersweet chocolate chips (optional)

Steps


  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C. Line a 20 × 20 cm pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, whisk melted butter, cocoa powder, and vanilla until smooth (no cocoa lumps). Set aside.
  3. In another bowl, beat eggs and brown sugar until light, fluffy, and pale.
  4. Fold the cocoa-butter mixture into the egg mixture until combined.
  5. Fold in flour and salt until no flour streaks remain.
  6. Gently fold in raspberries and chocolate chips (if using).
  7. Spread batter evenly into the prepared pan.
  8. Bake about 25 minutes, then start checking. Brownies are done when edges are set but a knife in the center still comes out with a bit of moist batter/crumbs (up to 35 minutes).
  9. Cool before slicing into squares and serving.
