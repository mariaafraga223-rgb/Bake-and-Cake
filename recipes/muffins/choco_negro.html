Double Chocolate Banana Muffins

Deep cocoa flavor, extra chocolate chips, and ripe banana sweetness — soft, rich, and bakery-style.

Prep 10 min | Cook 20 min | Total 30 min | Servings 10 muffins

Ingredients


  • 85 g unsalted butter, melted
  • 3 large overripe bananas, mashed
  • 200 g granulated sugar
  • 1 egg (room temperature)
  • 71 g Greek yogurt
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 125 g all-purpose flour
  • 43 g Dutch processed cocoa powder
  • 3/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 112 g chocolate chips

Steps


  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C. Grease a 10-cup muffin tin and line with paper liners.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the melted butter, mashed bananas, and sugar until combined.
  3. Add the egg and mix well.
  4. Add the Greek yogurt and vanilla extract. Mix to combine.
  5. Add flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Mix until just combined and no dry streaks remain.
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips (mix only until evenly distributed).
  7. Scoop batter into the muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full. Add a few extra chips on top if you want.
  8. Bake 5 minutes at 220°C, then reduce the oven to 190°C and bake 15 minutes more, until the tops spring back lightly to the touch.
  9. Cool at least 15 minutes before eating.
